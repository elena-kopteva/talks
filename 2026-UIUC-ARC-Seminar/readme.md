Title: Approaching Dirty Black Holes

Venue/Date: Astrophysics, Relativity, Cosmology (ARC) seminar, UIUC Physics, 2026

Astrophysical black holes are not isolated vacuum objects. They are embedded in surrounding matter and fields. The increasing precision of observations makes it essential to quantify how such environments influence gravitational and electromagnetic observables. This talk is about exact solutions for static, spherically symmetric black holes surrounded by anisotropic matter, with emphasis on Kiselev-type geometries as a simple, interpretable framework for studying environmental effects. We describe how the surrounding medium modifies horizon structure and the effective potential for a test scalar field, including recent results for charged massless scalar perturbations. We also outline how these solutions can be implemented in numerical relativity tools such as the Einstein Toolkit, providing a modular, analytically controlled testbed for probing environmental effects in the time domain.
